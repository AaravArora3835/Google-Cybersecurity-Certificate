## Incident Handler’s Journal — Entry 01

### **Date**

December 23, 2025

### **Entry**

1

### **Description**

This journal entry documents a ransomware security incident that impacted a small U.S. healthcare clinic. The incident resulted in encrypted systems, loss of access to patient data, and a complete shutdown of business operations due to a successful phishing attack.

---

### **Tool(s) Used**

No technical security tools were directly used during this phase. Information was gathered through employee reports, on-screen ransom messages, and incident observation.

---

### **The 5 W’s**

**Who caused the incident?**
The incident was caused by an organized group of unethical hackers known for targeting healthcare and transportation organizations using ransomware attacks.

**What happened?**
Attackers sent targeted phishing emails containing a malicious attachment. Once opened, malware was installed and ransomware was deployed, encrypting critical company files and displaying a ransom note demanding payment for a decryption key.

**When did the incident occur?**
The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m., when employees began reporting loss of access to systems and files.

**Where did the incident happen?**
The incident occurred within the internal network of a small U.S.-based healthcare clinic specializing in primary care services.

**Why did the incident happen?**
The incident happened because employees fell victim to a phishing email and downloaded a malicious attachment, allowing attackers to gain access and deploy ransomware due to insufficient email security awareness or technical controls.

---

### **Additional Notes**

This incident highlights the importance of phishing awareness training, email filtering, and regular system backups in healthcare environments. A key question is whether the organization had offline backups or an incident response plan in place prior to the attack.
